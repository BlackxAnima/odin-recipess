Garlic Steak

Garlic Steak: Juicy, flavorful cuts infused with robust garlic, seared to perfection for a mouthwatering experience.

This garlic steak recipe combines the bold flavors of garlic with succulent steak, creating a dish that's both simple and satisfying. To prepare, start by marinating your choice of steak with minced garlic , olive oil, salt, and pepper. Allow the steak to absorb the flavors for at least 30 minutes, ensuring each bite is infused with garlic goodness.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (about 900g) of steak (such as ribeye, sirloin, or New York strip)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: Fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary for garnish

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the steak in a shallow dish or resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over it, ensuring the steak is evenly coated.
  3. Cover or seal the dish/bag and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Preheat your grill or a skillet over medium-high heat. If using a skillet, add a bit of olive oil to coat the surface.
  5. Remove the steak from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off.
  6. Place the steak on the grill or skillet. Cook the steak for about 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare, adjusting the time according to your preferred level of doneness.
  7. For grill marks, rotate the steak 90 degrees halfway through cooking on each side.
  8. Once cooked to your liking, remove the steak from the grill or skillet and let it rest on a cutting board for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.
  9. Slice the steak against the grain into thin strips.
  10. Optionally,garnish with fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary.
